The Problem

African healthcare is broken at the infrastructure level

Not for lack of brilliant doctors or dedicated workers, but because the systems underneath don't talk to each other.

🗂️

Fragmented Records

A patient can visit four different clinics and each one starts from scratch. Medical history is stored on paper, in siloed systems, or not at all. Continuity of care is nearly impossible.

~90% of records are paper-based
🔌

Zero Interoperability

Hospitals, labs, pharmacies, and insurers operate on completely separate systems with no shared standards. Claims take weeks. Drug verifications fail. Referrals get lost.

No continent-wide data standard
📊

No Health Intelligence

Governments and health ministries make policy decisions with almost no real-time data. Outbreak detection is slow. Resource allocation is guesswork. The data layer simply doesn't exist.

Critical decisions made blind
The Solution

One platform. Every actor. One connected system.

Medicol is not another app. We are building the API layer that every actor in African healthcare plugs into, creating network effects, a data moat, and compounding value with every new participant.

Why Now

The conditions for this are finally right

Three forces are converging to make this the right moment to build Africa's health infrastructure layer.

$259B

The African healthcare market is projected to reach $259 billion by 2030 with almost no foundational data infrastructure built yet. The opportunity is wide open.

UHC

Across Africa, governments are pushing toward Universal Health Coverage. Every UHC program needs exactly what Medicol builds — an interoperable data layer to track, verify, and serve citizens.

4G+

Mobile penetration and 4G connectivity have reached critical mass across East and West Africa. For the first time, a software infrastructure layer is practically deployable at scale.
